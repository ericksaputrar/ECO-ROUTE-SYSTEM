Strategic Background & Key Issues

The rationale behind the Eco-Route program in Bandung City

Bandung City is currently in a prolonged waste emergency with daily volumes reaching 1,500 tons, resulting in a 786% Overcapacity at the Sarimukti Landfill. The failure of decentralization systems at the regional level is caused by crucial mid-level infrastructure like Sadang Serang TPS3R and local Waste Banks not being optimally utilized due to circular coordination constraints, micro-logistics limitations, and low public sorting literacy. High-economic-value waste is dumped directly into the landfill, paralyzing the circular green economy chain in the Coblong area.

Eco-Route Sustainable Solution: The system does not require the construction of new heavy physical infrastructure, but rather optimizes the remaining holding capacity of processing units and accelerates the downstream distribution integration of recycled products to local urban farming and creative industry practitioners in an integrated manner.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

End-to-end integration flow of Eco-Route circular waste processing

1

Source Sorting

Domestic/business sectors strictly sort organic & inorganic waste into specific QR-coded containers.

2

Smart Micro-Routing

Logistics receive the most optimal pickup route from the system algorithm to save fuel.

3

TPS3R Pre-Processing

Sadang Serang TPS3R performs load verification, mechanical shredding, and quality stabilization.

4

Community Conversion

Urban Farming/Recycling communities convert raw materials into mature compost, biogas, or creative products.

5

Circular Market

Products are ready to be resold through the circular marketplace platform to self-fund the ecosystem.
